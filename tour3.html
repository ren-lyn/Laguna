Colosseum, Rome

Description

The **Colosseum**, located in the heart of Rome, is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world. This ancient amphitheater, also known as the Flavian Amphitheater, was completed in AD 80 and once hosted gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. Today, it stands as a testament to Rome's architectural ingenuity and history.

The Colosseum could hold up to **50,000 spectators** and was the stage for brutal gladiatorial fights, animal hunts, and mock naval battles. It remains one of the largest and most well-preserved ancient structures in the world.

Key Highlights:
  • Explore the ancient gladiator arena
  • Discover the underground chambers where gladiators and animals were held
  • Visit the upper levels for panoramic views of Rome
  • Learn about the history through informative exhibitions
Visiting the Colosseum:

The Colosseum is open to visitors year-round, and guided tours are available to explore its vast history. Tickets grant access to the Colosseum, the Roman Forum, and Palatine Hill, offering a complete experience of ancient Rome.
