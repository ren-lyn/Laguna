Brief History of Laguna Province

Laguna Province, located in the CALABARZON region of the Philippines, holds deep historical and cultural significance. It was named after "La Laguna," meaning "the lake" in Spanish, referring to Laguna de Bay—the largest lake in the country. The province is notable for being the birthplace of the Philippine national hero, Dr. José Rizal, and was a center of revolutionary activity during the Spanish colonial era. Its rich heritage is reflected in its centuries-old churches, ancestral houses, and historical landmarks.
