Pila Heritage Town

A Glimpse into the Past


Pila's town center, laid out in the classic Spanish colonial "church-plaza-town hall" design, features a harmonious blend of historical structures. The Pila Municipal Center, constructed in 1931, stands as a prominent example of early 20th-century architecture, reflecting the town's rich heritage. The San Antonio de Padua Church, originally built in 1578 and reconstructed in the 19th century, serves as the spiritual heart of the community. Nearby, the Pila Museum houses artifacts from the Chinese and American periods, offering insights into the town's diverse cultural influences.
