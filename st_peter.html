Pakil Church (St. Peter of Alcantara Parish)

Discover the Sacred Beauty of Saint Peter of Alcantara Parish Church


A Historic Sanctuary in Pakil, Laguna Nestled in the picturesque town of Pakil, Laguna, the Saint Peter of Alcantara Parish Church stands as a testament to centuries of faith, resilience, and architectural brilliance. Established in 1676 under the patronage of Saint Peter of Alcantara, this historic church has withstood the test of time, enduring fires, earthquakes, and wars, emerging as one of the most revered religious sites in the Philippines. The church's architectural grandeur is a fusion of Corinthian and Ionic styles, featuring a cruciform structure measuring 162 feet in length and 36 feet in width. Its façade is adorned with classical columns and intricate stone reliefs, while the side entrance showcases elaborate designs typical of Laguna's colonial-era churches. The belfry, completed in 1777, houses five bells that resonate through the town, calling the faithful to prayer. Inside, the church boasts a main retablo painted in white, housing a pantheon of 14 saints, with the Archangel Michael towering above. A life-sized figure of a crucified Christ is displayed in a smaller retablo near the church entrance. The ceiling features a large painting depicting the concept of Heaven, Earth, Hell, and the Final Judgment, created by 19th-century artist Jose Dans. Adjacent to the church is a museum dedicated to the Nuestra Señora de los Dolores de Turumba, a 9 by 12-inch oil painting of the Virgin Mary discovered in Laguna de Bay in 1788. This miraculous image sparked the devotion of the Our Lady of Turumba, leading to the annual Turumba Festival—the largest and longest religious celebration in the country. The museum houses the original painting, along with clothes, perfumes, jewelry, and other historic memorabilia. Whether you're a pilgrim seeking spiritual solace or a traveler eager to explore the rich tapestry of Philippine history, a visit to Saint Peter of Alcantara Parish Church promises an enriching experience that connects the past with the present.
