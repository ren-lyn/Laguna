Statue of Liberty, NYC

Description

The Statue of Liberty, located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, is one of the most iconic symbols of the United States and freedom. It was a gift from France to commemorate the Centennial of the American Declaration of Independence and symbolize the friendship between the two nations.

Officially dedicated on **October 28, 1886**, the statue stands **305 feet (93 meters)** tall, including its pedestal, and is made of copper. Over time, its color has changed from metallic copper to its famous **green patina** due to oxidation.

Key Highlights:
  • Designed by French sculptor **Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi**, with internal structure by **Gustave Eiffel**.
  • The statue represents **Libertas**, the Roman goddess of freedom, holding a torch and a tablet inscribed with the date of the U.S. Declaration of Independence (July 4, 1776).
  • The **torch** symbolizes enlightenment, while the **crown** has **seven rays** representing the seven seas and continents.
  • Visitors can access the **pedestal** and **crown** (limited access) to enjoy panoramic views of New York City and the harbor.
  • The statue is a symbol of freedom and democracy, especially for immigrants arriving in the U.S. through Ellis Island.
Visiting the Statue of Liberty:

Visitors can take a ferry from **Battery Park** to **Liberty Island**, where the statue stands. There are different levels of access to the statue, including the **pedestal** and the **crown**. Limited tickets are available to access the crown. Liberty Island also offers a museum showcasing the history and significance of the statue.
