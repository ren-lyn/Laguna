Seven Lakes of San Pablo

Explore the Enchanting Seven Lakes of San Pablo


Nature, Culture, and Serenity in One Stunning Destination Embark on a journey through one of Laguna’s most captivating natural wonders—the Seven Lakes of San Pablo City. Nestled at the foot of Mounts Banahaw, Makiling, and the Sierra Madre range, these seven crater lakes—Sampaloc, Bunot, Palakpakin, Mohicap, Yambo, Pandin, and Calibato—offer a peaceful escape for travelers seeking scenic beauty, local culture, and eco-adventure. Each lake has its own unique charm. Whether it’s a bamboo raft ride and lakeside lunch at Pandin, a romantic view from the shores of Yambo, or catching the sunset at Sampaloc, the largest and most accessible of the seven—you’ll find endless opportunities for sightseeing, swimming, fishing, and nature photography. These volcanic lakes are not only breathtakingly beautiful but also deeply rooted in local folklore and history, making your visit both a visual and cultural experience. Reconnect with nature and discover the soul of San Pablo—a city blessed with the rare gift of seven natural lakes waiting to be explored.
